Abstract

Concrete is a widely used construction material known for its strength, durability, and versatility. Accurate prediction of concrete compressive strength is crucial for ensuring the safety and reliability of structures. In recent years, machine learning techniques, including neural networks, have gained prominence in predicting concrete properties. This article explores the application of neural network models and other machine learning algorithms for predicting the compressive strength of concrete. We discuss the methodology, data preprocessing, model training, and evaluation. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of these models in enhancing prediction accuracy and reducing the need for labor-intensive and time-consuming laboratory tests.
